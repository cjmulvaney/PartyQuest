# Party Quest — Mission Library v1

100 missions across 10 categories, 10 per category.
For use as seed data in the initial build.

---

## 1. Icebreakers
*Conversation starters and get-to-know-you prompts.*

1. Find someone who has lived in another country and ask them about it.
2. Learn the full name of someone you only know by first name tonight.
3. Find out what someone's most unusual job or side hustle has been.
4. Ask someone what their most unpopular opinion is.
5. Find someone whose birthday is in the same month as yours.
6. Ask someone what they wanted to be when they grew up — and whether they still want that.
7. Find out what one person's hidden talent is.
8. Ask someone what the last thing they googled was.
9. Find someone who has met a celebrity and hear the story.
10. Ask someone what the best piece of advice they've ever received was.

---

## 2. Silly Dares
*Low-stakes, goofy challenges designed to get laughs.*

1. Do your best impression of someone at the party without saying who it is. Let people guess.
2. Speak in a British accent for the next 5 minutes.
3. Do 10 jumping jacks in the middle of the room right now.
4. Find someone and challenge them to a 30-second staring contest.
5. Walk up to someone and compliment their shoes, regardless of what they're wearing.
6. Moonwalk across the room.
7. Say "hashtag" out loud before every sentence for the next 3 minutes.
8. Do your best runway walk from one end of the room to the other.
9. Call someone by the wrong name for the rest of the night. See how long it takes them to notice.
10. Convince someone to high-five a stranger with you.

---

## 3. Storytelling
*Get people sharing — personal stories, memories, and moments.*

1. Tell someone a story about the most embarrassing thing that's ever happened to you.
2. Get someone to tell you about their worst travel experience.
3. Share the story of how you met the host of this event.
4. Find out about someone's most memorable meal.
5. Ask someone about the best decision they ever made on a whim.
6. Get someone to tell you about a time they were completely lost — literally or figuratively.
7. Share a story about a time you got in trouble as a kid.
8. Ask someone what their earliest memory is.
9. Find out about a time someone did something they were really proud of but never got credit for.
10. Tell someone about the most spontaneous thing you've ever done.

---

## 4. Food & Drink
*Missions involving beverages, snacks, or culinary moments.*

1. Get someone to make a toast — to anything. It must be at least 3 sentences.
2. Find someone willing to try a food they've never had before tonight (if available).
3. Convince someone to switch drinks with you for one sip.
4. Ask the bartender or host what their go-to drink order is.
5. Create a new cocktail name on the spot and pitch it to someone like it's a real menu item.
6. Find someone and agree on the best and worst bar snack of all time.
7. Get someone to cheers you using a word other than "cheers."
8. Challenge someone to describe their drink using only three words.
9. Ask someone what their signature dish is and get the recipe.
10. Find out what someone's most controversial food opinion is (e.g. pineapple on pizza).

---

## 5. Performance
*Sing, dance, act, demonstrate — put yourself out there.*

1. Sing the first verse of any song you know by heart to one person.
2. Teach someone a dance move — any move you actually know.
3. Do a 30-second stand-up bit. It doesn't have to be funny.
4. Perform a dramatic reading of a text message from your phone.
5. Act out a movie scene using only gestures. No words. The other person has to guess the movie.
6. Beatbox for 15 seconds in front of at least two people.
7. Perform your best air guitar solo to whatever song is playing.
8. Recite something you have memorized — poem, speech, movie quote, song chorus — anything.
9. Do a dramatic slow-motion walk across the room.
10. Freestyle rap for 15 seconds. Any topic. Any skill level.

---

## 6. Social Connector
*Find commonalities, make introductions, build bridges.*

1. Introduce two people who don't know each other and find one thing they have in common.
2. Find someone who grew up in the same state or region as you.
3. Find someone who shares a niche hobby or interest with you — something most people wouldn't guess.
4. Introduce yourself to someone using only three words to describe who you are.
5. Find two people at the party who should know each other and explain why.
6. Find someone who has read one of your favorite books.
7. Find someone who grew up watching the same TV show you did.
8. Ask someone to introduce you to one person they think you'd get along with.
9. Find someone who has been to a country you want to visit and ask for a recommendation.
10. Find someone who shares your Myers-Briggs or enneagram type (or is your opposite).

---

## 7. Bold Dares
*Higher commitment, higher stakes — for the brave.*

1. Ask a stranger at the party to be your plus-one for a group photo. Actually take the photo.
2. Give a 60-second motivational speech to whoever is standing closest to you.
3. Go around to five people and tell each one something genuinely specific you noticed about them.
4. Ask someone you don't know well what they think your vibe is at first glance. Listen without defending yourself.
5. Text someone outside the party right now and tell them you're thinking about them.
6. Publicly announce your most controversial hot take to a group of at least three people.
7. Ask someone for honest, unsolicited feedback on something — a decision you made, an idea you have, anything.
8. Find the person at this party you know the least and spend 5 minutes asking them questions only.
9. Tell someone something you've been meaning to say but haven't gotten around to.
10. Ask someone to roast you — one minute, no holding back. You cannot respond.

---

## 8. Deep Cut
*Meaningful, thoughtful prompts for genuine human connection.*

1. Ask someone what they would do differently if they knew no one would judge them.
2. Find out what someone's biggest non-career ambition is.
3. Ask someone what the hardest thing they've ever had to learn was.
4. Find out what someone is most looking forward to in the next year.
5. Ask someone what they think their purpose is — even if they're still figuring it out.
6. Ask someone about a moment that changed how they see the world.
7. Find out what someone misses most about being a kid.
8. Ask someone what they wish more people understood about them.
9. Find out what someone would tell their younger self if they could.
10. Ask someone what they think the secret to a good life is.

---

## 9. Party Classic
*Timeless party game energy — familiar, inclusive, high participation.*

1. Start a game of two truths and a lie with whoever is nearest to you.
2. Get a group of at least four people to play a round of "never have I ever."
3. Challenge someone to a thumb war. Best of three.
4. Find someone willing to play rock paper scissors — loser buys the next round or owes a favor.
5. Get a group of three or more to play 21 questions with you as the subject.
6. Start a chain compliment — you compliment someone, they have to immediately compliment someone else, see how long it goes.
7. Challenge someone to a coin flip. Loser has to do whatever the winner says (within reason).
8. Get at least five people to participate in a group "most likely to" question of your choosing.
9. Start a round of "would you rather" with at least two other people.
10. Organize an impromptu trivia question — you ask, anyone can answer, first correct answer wins.

---

## 10. Wildcard
*Unexpected, chaotic, hard to categorize. High entropy.*

1. Find out what someone's secret talent is that they never get to show off.
2. Ask someone to teach you something useful in under two minutes.
3. Find someone who has a scar and hear the story behind it (only if they're comfortable sharing).
4. Get someone to tell you their most-used emoji and what it says about them.
5. Find out what someone's karaoke song would be — the one they'd actually nail.
6. Ask someone what they would do with $10,000 they had to spend in 24 hours.
7. Find out what app on someone's phone they would be most embarrassed to explain.
8. Ask someone what the weirdest thing they believe is.
9. Find out what someone's most irrational fear is.
10. Ask someone what they think happens after we die. Get their actual answer.
